The High Holy Days begin early this year, and CBI is kicking off our annual appeal. I am instituting an online pledge opportunity for the first time!  If successful, I will present the “State of the Congregation”, but not make an appeal during Kol Nidre services.  I ask you to make both financial and spiritual pledges as we renew ourselves in the New Year.

Financial Pledge: As many of you may not be aware, CBI must rely on fundraising activities such as the High Holy Day Appeal in order to maintain our current level of programming and services. Membership dues, school tuition, and other fees combined do not cover our operating expenses. The success of this appeal relies on the generosity of our members and guests. Financial pledges may be paid by credit card or check. 

Spiritual Pledge: CBI depends on the commitment of each one of us to keep our community strong and growing.  As Rosh Hashannah approaches, I ask that each of us pledge to taking on at least one additional mitzvah or deed in 5774.  Spiritual pledges are for our personal and communal growth, and each of us will be responsible for fulfilling our own pledge.  The clergy and lay leaders are happy to help you.

My wish is that each of us will contribute financially as generously as we can, and spiritually in a way that is personally meaningful. I am hoping to stand before our congregation during the High Holy Days and be able to say that 100% of our incredible community contributed and participated in this annual tradition. Every pledge matters and makes a difference.  Thank you for your support. 

Sincerely,
Beth Elster
CBI President
